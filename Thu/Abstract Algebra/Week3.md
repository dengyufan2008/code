## P85 T1

Let $\phi : G \rarr H$ be a homomorphism and let $E$ be a subgroup of $H$. Prove that $\phi^{-1}(E) \le G$. If $E \unlhd H$ prove that $\phi^{-1}(E) \unlhd G$. Deduce that $\ker \phi \unlhd G$.

---

Since $1 \in E$, then $1 \in \phi^{-1}(E)$. For all $x, y \in \phi^{-1}(E)$, we have $\phi(xy^{-1}) = \phi(x)\phi(y)^{-1} \in E$ since $\phi(x), \phi(y) \in E$. So $xy^{-1} \in \phi^{-1}(E)$, $\phi^{-1}(E) \le G$.

To prove $\phi^{-1}(E) \unlhd G$, we only need to prove $g \phi^{-1}(E) g^{-1} \subseteq \phi^{-1}(E)$. For all $x \in \phi^{-1}(E)$, we have $\phi(gxg^{-1}) = \phi(g) \phi(x) \phi(g)^{-1} \in E$ since $\phi(x) \in E$, $E \unlhd H$.

## P88 T24

Prove that if $N \unlhd G$ and $H$ is any subgroup of $G$ then $N \cap H \unlhd H$.

---

Since $N \le G$, $H \le G$, then $N \cap H$ is a group. Since $N \cap H \subseteq H$, then $N \cap H \le H$.

We need to prove $g(N \cap H)g^{-1} \subseteq N \cap H$. For all $x \in N \cap H$, $g \in H$, we have $gxg^{-1} \in N$ since $N \unlhd G$. We also have $gxg^{-1} \in H$ since $x, g \in H$. So $gxg^{-1} \in N \cap H$.

## P96 T18

Let $G$ be a finite group, let $H$ be a subgroup of $G$ and let $N \unlhd G$. Prove that if $|H|$ and $|G:N|$ are relatively prime then $H \le N$.

---

Suppose $x \in H$, but $x \notin N$. We have $p=|x|$ divides $|H|$, $q=|xN|$ divides $|G:N|$. Since $(|H|, |G:N|)=1$, we have $(p, q)=1$.

Since $x^p=1$, then $(xN)^p=x^pN=1N=N$. So $p \mid q$, thus $p=1$.

So we have $x^1=1$, thus $x=1$. But we know $x \notin N$, contradiction.

So $H \subseteq N$. And $H$ is a group, then $H \le N$.
